Statistics, basic concepts, variables.
Institistics, variables are numbers or characteristics that can be counted or measured.
For example, age, length and weight could be variables, even the height of a building.
Each of these numbers represents a variable which can change or take on different values.
For example, Lucas has just met the five newest members of his family.
He wants to keep an eye on their health, so he starts recording their weight.
Kiyara is the smallest of the litter, weighing 4.5 pounds.
Then there's Simba, whose five pounds.
Rocky, 4.8.
Nala, 4.6, and Bruno, whose five.
In this case, the variable is weight, which changes depending on the dog.
If Lucas wanted to track their growth more closely, he could measure other variables like
their height or length, giving him a larger data set, which just means more information.
These types of variables are called quantitative variables, which can be expressed
are reflected in numbers.
If there are also qualitative variables, too, which are characteristics that can only
be expressed in words.
For people, it might be things like ethnicity, profession, or gender.
If Lucas decided to replace his quantitative variables with qualitative ones, he could swap
the numbers for traits like the dog's code or eye color.
These variables are descriptive, using words rather than numbers.
As you can see, there are different types of variables we can consider when collecting data.
